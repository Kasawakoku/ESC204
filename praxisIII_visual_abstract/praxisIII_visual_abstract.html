BARRY

Smart Roof System Prototype

Opportunity: Passive thermal regulation for RDP Homes in Johannesburg.
Stakeholder Focus: Improving liveability for Occupants while meeting Policy energy goals.

Design Realization

Full Design Concept Summary:

  1. Subsystems: Control, Actuation, Sensors, Ventilation
  2. Full-scale roof coverage (m²) with corresponding support system capabilities
  3. Aluminum treads containing Parrafin Wax phase change passive heat absorbtion
  4. Fiber glass insulation layer

Created Prototype Comparison:

  1. Subsystems: Electrical, Software, Mechanical (Ventilation Scoped Out)
  2. Small Scale Belt with 0.263x0.381m dimensions using NEMA 17 motor
  3. Laser-cut wood treads without Parrafin Wax phase change passive heat absorbtion
  4. 1.2cm Thickness Foam insulation layer

Magnified Area: Key Mechanical Design Features

1

Metal Axle Upgrade

Iterated from wooden axles to withstand the high torque from the NEMA 17 stepper motor, ensuring system durability.

2

2.5mm TPU Timing Belt

Custom printed with 50% infill to balance pulley flexibility with structural thickness for load-bearing capabilities.

Low-Profile Stitching

Fishing wire knots are kept outside the belt to maximize contact patch and prevent tooth slippage on the pulleys.

4

Drilled Wood Treads

Pivot from failed TPU glue bonding. Anchor holes were drilled into rigid wood treads instead of elastic TPU belts.

Opportunity Context

Johannesburg RDP Occupant
Needs passive thermal regulation to maintain comfort during 10-15°C diurnal swings.
Policy (South African Government)
Mandates reduction in building grid energy demand.
Operator (Companies and Contractors)
Requires durable, low-maintenance systems.

Performance & Impact

Occ Opr Pol

Thermal Control (S-1.1.1 & S-1.2.1)

Occ Pol

Triggers heating at <21°C and cooling at >24°C. Maintains 18-27°C comfort zone despite 3-30°C external swings.

Responsiveness (S-1.1.2 & S-1.1.3)

Opr

Accurately detects >2°C shifts. Mode switch completes in 8.4s (Target: < 1 min), well within daily cycle needs.

Efficiency & Noise (S-2.1.1 & S-3.2.1)

Pol Occ

Draws < 57kJ/day grid energy (comparable to a 2W fan). Switches quietly at 18 dBA (Target: < 20 dBA).

Autonomy Outcome (S-3.1.1)

Opr Occ

System operates for 10+ day/night cycles without manual user intervention, achieving low-maintenance operation.

Holistic Impact Assessment:

BARRY aligns Technology Developers (cost-effective TPU/Wood) with Occupant Needs (comfort/quiet) and Policy Makers (passive heating/cooling) to create a feasible sustainability solution.
